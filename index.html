How to make a GIF in Adobe Photoshop:

Assembly time: 20 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Are you looking to make a GIF in Adobe Photoshop? It's super easy and fun to do! Follow these steps and you'll be able to make your own GIF!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Computer
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Video

  1. Find the video you would like to convert into a GIF and then open Photoshop.

    You can shorten the length of the video within Photoshop, so it is not necessary to edit the video length prior to starting. Remember! The longer the video, the larger the GIF file will be and certain platforms have limits on how big files can be.

  2. Create a new document the size of your choice.

    The documentt size can be whatever pixel or inch length you need or want it to be.

  3. Transfer the video into a series of individual images.

    In Photoshop, go to File > Import > Video Frames to Layers. Find the video you want to convert into a GIF and select it. Choose the range of the video you would like to import; you can either select the entire video or a specific time range. Remember! The longer the video you import, the larger the file will be! Make sure “Make Frame Animation” is selected and click “okay”.

  4. Crop the GIF if necessary.

    If your inserted video is too large, use the Crop tool to edit the size.

  5. Select how long you would like the GIF to loop.

    Click the repeat menu and you can decide how often you would like the GIF to loop, click forever if you would like a looping animation.

  6. Save your GIF.

    Then you are going to export the animation as a GIF. Click File > Export > Save For Web (Legacy). Click on preset and choose “GIF 128 Dithered”. While you can choose any of the other GIF options, the smaller the number, the less colors will be saved impacting how the GIF appears. And an undithered GIF will lose the illusion of continuous colors. From there, select preview to make sure your GIF is how you want it. Then save to your computer.

  7. Making a GIF in Adobe Photoshop 2018.
